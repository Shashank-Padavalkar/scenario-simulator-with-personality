# Decision Frameworks
The OODA Loop (Observe, Orient, Decide, Act) is a four-step approach to decision-making that focuses on filtering available information, putting it in context, and quickly making the most appropriate decision.
First Principles Thinking involves breaking down a problem to its fundamental truths and building up from there. It avoids reasoning by analogy.
Inversion involves thinking about how a decision could go perfectly wrong and then working backwards to avoid those outcomes.
